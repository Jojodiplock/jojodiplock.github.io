Google Maps

UX design review project

Overview

This project was derived from a problem expressed by people who are looking for a safer and more accessible ways of getting where they need to go. Where do you turn when you need to find a route through the busy city? What if there was a place where you could find your ideal path with helpful recommendations that insure your safety.

My role

Tools

Summary

Problem

Safety and longing for reassurance is always on ones mind when traveling especially in a new area. The need for a reliable source to guide your path is hard to come by. Goggle maps is an app that over a billion people each month use the discover new places and navigate their way around. There are still opportunities for it to evolve and enhance the user experience within the realm of safety and bikers convenience.

Outcome

Enhance users happiness and loyalty when using Google Maps. Improve navigation accuracy, location tracking, and personalized route recommendations, ensuring a safer, more efficient experience. Prioritize the needs of cyclists by incorporating bike lanes, topographical maps, and safer route options. Add personalized place recommendations according to users searches.

End Result

Final design deliverable is 4 key ideas to improve google maps:

Personalized customization options for routes

Including local events and activities recombinations

Topographic map layer

Detailed bike route filter

Mockups

Personalized customization options for routes

Using the customize bottom a user can make route preferences specific to them but turning toggles on and off. This simple quick way of customizing a route will allow users to experiences seamless with no surprises.

Including local events and activities recombinations

Locals are more likely to use google maps latest in the area page when it is constantly changing with new places and events. Events will allow users to get the most out of their community.

Topographic map layer

Users can not always tell what is a hill and what is flat so a topographical map will play a big role in picking a route especially when walking or on a bike.

Detailed bike route filter

A Bike layer that shows different bike route options allows users to visualize where that are going to bike and what the path might look like. A road bike might not want to go on a gravel trail and choose a different path.

The Problem

Dangerous bike routes

It can be unclear if a bike route is shared with cars or separate from the roads. It can be an unexpected and unsafe surprise when a user is told to go down a gravel road without the right bike. Choosing the right route becomes a chore to the users as they examine their entire route.

Unexpected bike routes

Capturing hills and valleys on a make is hard when there is no elevation markers along the way. How steep a hill is can determine wether or not a route will be chosen.

Lack of personalized customization

Being shown the same locations and recommendations with no change can be boring. Without change there is no need to go on to google maps.

Limited place recommendations

There is an abundance of information and reviews about places on google maps but users express that Google maps always shows them big box stores as apposed to a more personalized algorithm.

The Opportunity

Provide Comfort

There is an opportunity to create safe positive experience for people using google maps. People deserve to travel and ride there bike on safe accessible routes. Having access to a personalized algorithm on google maps will open the doors to meaningful experiences.

Research

Key Findings

After conducting interviews I organized users responses using an affinity map and feature prioritization to identify main themes such as pain points that I should focus on.

My analysis reviled that I will need to focus on safety and convenience especially when biking.

Personas and Interview themes

User flow: Choosing a Bike route

User flow: Exploring bike route layers and topographic map layers

User flow: Finding something to do in the area

Reflection

What I learned

This project reinforced the importance of designing for safety, accessibility, and personalization in navigation tools. Through user research, I gained a deeper understanding of the challenges cyclists and travellers face when navigating unfamiliar areas. I learned that clear route customization, elevation awareness, and personalized recommendations can significantly enhance user confidence and experience. Most importantly, this project highlighted the value of user-centred design and iterative problem-solving in creating meaningful, impactful solutions.
